Dear recruitment team,

I am writing in response to your advertisement for the PhD vacancy in 3D tomography of nano-samples. I believe my experience, skills and qualification are not only an asset, but an ideal match to the requirements of your operation.

I currently live in Göttingen, where I very recently finished my M.Sc. studies in Applied Computer Science at the Georg-August University. My topic of specialization is Scientific Computing. During my studies I took three consecutive courses on Mathematical Signal and Image Processing at the Institute of Numerical and Applied Mathematics, the last one of which taught computer tomography. I am therefore not only well-acquainted with Fourier analysis, but also with the Radon transform, which is the historical foundation of modern computer tomography.

Presently, I work at the Institute of Propulsion Technology at the German Aerospace Center, where I wrote my master's thesis in cooperation with my university. In the course of this thesis project about programming a turbine blade cooling geometry generator called CoolingGen, I could prove my deep mathematical understanding of linear algebra and my implementation talent in C++ and Python. To assess the quality of certain features of the software, CoolingGen's creation also required me to communicate with aerospace engineers, which was always a productive yet pleasant experience.
